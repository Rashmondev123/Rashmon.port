DevSDRContentMar 16, 2026

How SDR Lessons Improved My Portfolio

I almost launched my portfolio with nothing but screenshots. Then I remembered something from my SDR work: nobody buys features — they buy outcomes.

Using SDR principles, I reframed each project with a clear structure: Problem → Solution → Tech → Outcome. Suddenly, the same projects told stories that mattered to recruiters and clients.

One version says "I made a restaurant website." The other says "I built a full landing page for a Lagos food brand with menu filtering, gallery, and location sections — designed to convert visitors into reservations."

The code didn't change. The framing did. Lesson: storytelling and context matter as much as code.

ContentDevSDRMar 17, 2026

Writing Websites That Actually Convert

Building a website is one thing. Communicating its value is another.

By combining content strategy with development, I design interfaces that not only look good but guide users to action. Think of it as copy + code + strategy — your product speaks and sells even before you hit submit.

The most overlooked part of any website isn't the design — it's the words. A beautiful layout with weak copy converts nobody. Strong copy with average design still moves people.

My approach: understand the user first, then build the interface around what they need to feel to take action.

Web3SDRMarketingMar 18, 2026

Web3, SDR, and Content: Why They Belong Together

Web3 startups often struggle with visibility and trust. The tech is often solid — but the messaging? Confusing, jargon-heavy, and aimed at nobody in particular.

Using SDR and content principles, I research audiences, craft narratives, and design messaging that attracts, educates, and converts. My work combines technical skill, writing, and sales strategy — so Web3 projects don't just exist, they grow.

SDR taught me how to find the right people and open doors. Content taught me how to keep them interested. Dev gives me the ability to build the room they walk into. That's the full picture.
